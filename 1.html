Aime leon Dore

Aimé Leon Dore is a fashion and lifestyle brand based in Queens, New York, founded in March 2014.
Led by founder Teddy Santis, Aimé Leon Dore has their flagship store located on Mulberry Street in Manhattan's Nolita neighborhood

In 2015, the brand collaborated with Puma on an update of Puma's classic State sneaker.
In 2016, ALD opened its first physical retail location on Mott St. in New York's Nolita neighborhood.
The shop quickly developed a reputation for its strong community, evolving interior (which changed with each seasonal collection),
and its mysterious exterior (with curtains hung over the door and street-facing windows).
In 2017, Aimé Leon Dore collaborated with KITH on hoodies, t-shirts, pullover sailing jackets, pants, and caps
with a nautical theme and anchors, boat paddles, and evil eye iconography inspired by Mykonos.

In 2019, ALD moved to a larger storefront on nearby Mulberry St, That also has an attached nostalgic coffee shop.
The new store gave greater expression to the brand's creative vision and included a living-room style interior evoking classic Parisian homes,
an installation from New York artist Tyrrell Winston, as a well as Café Leon Dore, a Mediterranean coffee shop inspired by Santis' Greek roots with outdoor seating.
Jon Caramanica of The New York Times reviewed ALD's Mulberry flagship in his column "Critical Shopper."
The piece described ALD as a post-streetwear heritage brand.
